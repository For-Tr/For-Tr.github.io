戈尔巴乔夫去世：孤胆英雄与罪魁祸首的两极人生

2022.09.05
罗兰
难度：CSE9
课程导读

当地时间8月30日，苏联最后一位领导人戈尔巴乔夫因病去世，终年91岁。1985年，他出任苏联最高领导人，大力推行“戈尔巴乔夫改革”，加剧了苏联国内政治经济形势的恶化，最终导致苏联解体。戈尔巴乔夫推行了怎样的政策？为何俄罗斯和西方国家对他的评价大相径庭？一起来听今天的讲解。

英文原文

Mikhail Gorbachev, Reformer of Soviet Union and Its Last Leader, Dies at 91

By Ann M. Simmons

As the eighth and final leader of the Soviet Union, Mikhail Gorbachev sought to reform the state. But his efforts unleashed a wave of unstoppable forces that led to the nation’s demise, reshaping the geopolitical landscape and leaving the U.S. as the world’s sole superpower.

Russia’s state news agencies cited Moscow’s central clinical hospital as saying he had died aged 91.

The son of peasants, the world would come to know him as the architect of “perestroika” and “glasnost”—restructuring and openness—domestic policies he hoped would breathe new life into the country’s sluggish 1980s economy, remake the political system at a time of warming relations with the West.

Mr. Gorbachev ultimately resigned on Dec. 25, 1991. The next day, the U.S.S.R. was formally dissolved.

Mr. Gorbachev’s life following the end of his presidency was characterized by rounds on the lecture circuit in the West, penning papers and books, and hobnobbing with international dignitaries, who respected and admired him, including former British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher.

At home, he was relegated to the sidelines of politics, watching as many of the democratic reforms which he had spearheaded were diluted.

On the global stage, Mr. Gorbachev was viewed as a maverick compared with his predecessors. He cemented the thawing of the Cold War with the signing of the landmark Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty with President Ronald Reagan.

“Mr. Gorbachev deserves most of the credit,” Mr. Reagan later said.
