变穷酸的中产：财富自由梦终究破灭？

2022.08.10
林禹
难度：CSE6
课程导读

最近，一条“德国中产排队领救济粮”的新闻登上各大平台热搜。其实，在全球，中产家庭都是受到疫情、市场衰退、物价上涨等影响最大的一个群体；而富人和穷人承受的损失反而更小。中产阶级为何抗风险能力最差？他们该如何应对自己的经济危机？一起来听今天的讲解。

英文原文

The Upper Middle Class Is Getting Squeezed

By Dion Rabouin

Mark Yu had a profitable pandemic. Like many Americans, he added to his savings and pulled in big gains from the stock-market rally.

But 2022 hasn’t been so kind. His expenses have grown because of higher costs for gas, groceries and the dog food for his four German shepherds. The value of his stockholdings is shrinking. A cracked foundation in his new house cost tens of thousands of dollars to repair.

Over the first three months of 2022, upper-middle-class families lost a bigger chunk of their stock portfolios than the people who make more than them, according to the Federal Reserve. The value of their liabilities grew by 2% in this year’s first quarter, more than any other group, as they took on greater debt for auto loans, credit cards and other consumer credit.

While poorer families might feel the effects of inflation more deeply, they also have had the biggest wage increases and have the smallest share of their net wealth invested in financial markets. The richest families, meanwhile, have been hurt by market losses but have been insulated from the worst of inflation.

Mr. Yu, the Texas physical therapist, is trying to get through this year. He is taking on extra shifts, often working 6 a.m. to 8 p.m. He dipped into his savings and is considering borrowing against his investments in his brokerage account. “It’s a low mood right now,” Mr. Yu said. “But I’m optimistic.”

生词好句

1.the upper middle class

上中产阶级
拓展:
根据美联储（Federal Reserve）的定义，上中产阶级一般指学历较高（如具有硕士及以上学历），同时家庭年收入水平在75000-127000美元的群体。

the upper mid-range electric cars 中等偏上的电动汽车

2.squeeze

英 [skwiːz] 美 [skwiːz]
v. 压榨，挤压，用力按压；用力拥抱（hug tight）；遇到财务麻烦，减少开销
拓展:
Enjoy the fresh orange juice - squeezed with love!
享受这杯橙汁儿，它饱含了我的爱！
She squeezed her mom tight and said “take care”.
她用力抱住了自己的母亲并道”珍重”。

squeeze n. 财务麻烦，减少开销
During the debt crisis, the governments need a sharp squeeze to alleviate the hardships.
在债务危机中，政府需要大幅缩减预算来度过难关。

3.add to

增加（increase）

4.pull in big gains

赚取大把的钱财（earn a huge amount of money）
拓展:
pull in 赚钱，大把捞钱（earn money）

5.rally

英 [ˈræli] 美 [ˈræli]
n. （股市术语）股价回升；汽车拉力赛
拓展:
The Chinese Concept Stocks led a rally across the US market.
中概股在美国股市止跌回升。

6.grocery

英 [ˈɡrəʊsəri] 美 [ˈɡroʊsəri]
n. 杂货，日用品

7.shrink

英 [ʃrɪŋk] 美 [ʃrɪŋk]
v. （价值）缩水

8.foundation

英 [faʊnˈdeɪʃn] 美 [faʊnˈdeɪʃn]
n. 基础（premise）；房屋地基

9.stock portfolio

股票组合（stockholding）
拓展:
portfolio n. 公文包；一系列事物（比如，作品集、产品组合）
Bytedance has a superb portfolio.
字节跳动拥有非常强悍的产品矩阵。

10.Federal Reserve

美联储

11.liability

英 [ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti] 美 [ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti]
n. 负债（正式用语，强调负债状态所对应的法律义务）

12.take on debt

加杠杆
拓展:
Managing payment can be tricky if you take on too much debt.
如果你加了太高的杠杆，你管理自己的支付能力就会有点难。

debt-to-income ratio 债务收入比
A debt-to-income ratio less than 36% is considered safe.
债务收入比低于36%被认为是安全的债务水平。

13.feel the effects of inflation more deeply

更深刻地感受到通胀
拓展:
more perceptible 更可感知的

14.net wealth

净资产

15.insulate

英 [ˈɪnsjəleɪt] 美 [ˈɪnsəleɪt]
v. 隔绝，隔热，隔音，绝缘
拓展:
insulation n. 隔绝，隔热，隔音，绝缘
This pair of earbuds have great acoustic insulation.
这副入耳式耳机的隔音效果非常出色。

16.physical therapist

理疗师
拓展:
general practitioner 家庭医生（GP）

17.get through

度过难关，通过考验
拓展:
He did whatever needed to get through the test.
为了通过这场考验，他做了所有能做的。

18.take on extra shifts

额外加班

19.dip

英 [dɪp] 美 [dɪp]
v. 浸，蘸；少量地取出

20.borrow against

以……（资产）作为抵押来借钱
拓展:
She borrowed money against property to start a business.
她以房产为抵押借钱创业。

21.brokerage account

经纪账户（委托理财机构帮助投资时，用于放入投资资金的账户）
拓展:
brokerage n. 佣金；中间人业务
